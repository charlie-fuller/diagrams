Stage 1

Right now context lives in your head, scattered across Slack threads, and in meeting notes you'll never see again. This gives you one place where your work lives and one interface that reads all of it — so you stop spending mental energy on retrieval and start spending it on judgment.

  • Ask Claude what's open, what was decided, what needs to happen — against your actual work
  • Turn a raw transcript into a structured meeting note in seconds
  • Draft Slack messages, prep for meetings, surface forgotten context — from one place
  • Have a system that travels with you regardless of employer or machine

Stage 2
Your Tools Come to You

Every tool switch is a tax on thinking. You open Jira, lose your place, switch to Glean, lose your thread. After Stage 2, Jira and Glean come to you — one conversation pulls tickets, searches agents, creates actions, and cross-references your notes. The synthesis is handled. What's left is the call.

  • Pull your Jira board and surface what's actually due — without opening Jira
  • Create and update tickets from a conversation
  • Search Glean, review agent inventory, cross-reference against your notes
  • Get a pre-meeting brief that combines notes, tickets, and Glean content in one ask

03
Stage 3
GitHub + Memory — Your Work Has a Permanent Home

Your setup exists on one machine and Claude forgets everything between sessions. Memory and GitHub solve this together — decisions stick, context carries over, and your entire system is backed up to the internet and cloneable anywhere. You do all of it in plain language inside the CLI. You never touch the GitHub website.

  • Push your vault to GitHub from inside Claude Code — no browser needed
  • Have Claude carry decisions and preferences from one session to the next
  • Set up your own config repo (modeled on Charlie's clode) as personal infrastructure
  • Get your Anthropic API key — needed here and for the meeting enrichment in Stage 4

04
Stage 4
Meetings Enrich Themselves

Meetings are where most knowledge work happens and where most of it gets lost. After Stage 4, every meeting you record becomes a structured intelligence document automatically — decisions, actions, signals, and connections to everything else you're working on. The meeting note in your vault isn't a transcript. It's an analysis. This is where the system starts compounding.

  • Record a meeting; a fully enriched note appears in your vault within minutes
  • See automatically how a meeting connects to other things in your portfolio
  • Ask "what did we decide about X this month" and get a real answer
  • Never write a meeting note by hand again
Granola Granola Sync Auto-enrichment pipeline Charlie sets up the technical parts
05
Stage 5
Repeatable Workflows

You've been doing things manually that could be one prompt. Slash commands turn your most common workflows into single words — standup, meeting brief, action item extraction, daily focus. And your entire setup is now in a GitHub repo you own, cloneable on any machine in minutes. You stop rebuilding. You continue.

  • Run /today for your Jira agenda + open notes in one shot
  • Run /meeting [person] for a pre-meeting brief in seconds
  • Run /process-meeting to extract actions and push them to Jira
  • Clone your setup on a new machine — everything back in minutes
  • Start building your own commands for workflows unique to your job
/today /meeting /jira-standup /process-meeting /think /glean-agent clode config repo
